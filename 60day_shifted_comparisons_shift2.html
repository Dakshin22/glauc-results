60-Day VR vs Humphry Comparisons

This sheet shows the 10 patient-eye comparisons in the 60-day cohort after spatial alignment. It uses the current workflow: whitelist applied, VR quality filtering applied, and bilateral-only matching. Each row shows the original VR field, the shifted VR field in its raw scale, the shifted field after conversion to dB, the Humphry reference field, and an overlap panel that puts the mapped VR value and Humphry value in the same cell for direct comparison.

The alignment rule in this sheet uses flips and shifts from -2 to +2 cells, choosing the transform with the highest correlation × sqrt(overlap).

This pooled view uses one shared VR-to-dB mapping across the whole cohort so every row is directly comparable visually.

Raw VR Colors

Lower non-blind VR values, closer to better sensitivity.
Higher non-blind VR values, closer to worse sensitivity.
`1.0`, the VR blind value.

Mapped dB Colors

Lower dB, worse Humphry sensitivity.
Higher dB, better Humphry sensitivity.
`-1`, the Humphry blind value.

Overlap Colors

Very close match, error `<= 3 dB`.
Moderate match, error `<= 5 dB`.
Loose match, error `<= 8 dB`.
Poor match, error `> 8 dB` or blind/non-blind disagreement.
